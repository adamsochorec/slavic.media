It must be at least five years since I inherited a collection of analogue lens (yes, analogue is the right term, unlike vintage) from a grandfather of my family friends. As the title and former lens owner might suggest, our subject of focus is not from this century, but rather from the second half of the previous one. We will be taking a look at the marvelous and spoiled **Flektogon 35mm f/2.4**, **Helios 44-2 58mm f/2** and **Orestor 100mm f/2.8**. 

![](https://cdn.slavic.media/img//4K "")

Each and every of this holy trinity of lenses has a very specific and unmistakable look. The most recognisable aspects are probably their unique bokeh, focus depth and behavior against direct sunlight.

:br 
> In photography, bokeh is the aesthetic quality of the blur produced in out-of-focus parts of an image, whether foreground or background or both. It is created by using a wide aperture lens. :br -[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bokeh)

The best way to understand why, is this old junk worth our attention is to take a look at some visual examples. In the demonstration video bellow I tested mentioned lenses with our main camera _Sony A7 IV_. Because the bajonet mount of all of the lenses is basically just a screw, I had to use the M42 to E-mount convertor. There are contacts for autofocus, exposure or appeture, just a simple screw. Combination of a contemporary mirriorless camera, external monitor and analogue lenses is particulary a remamarkable experience.

:br 

Avalabile focus peaking and decent screen size of Shinobi 5" from Atomos brings allows you to appreciate the charm of the delightful analogue lens bokeh and colour rendering in a real time, instead of having to wait for the post production, as I had to back in the days, when I only had a Canon 6D - DLSR witout any focus support. One of the main reasons you might apprecieate a larger display or focus peaking is the expectionally shallow depth of focus. 
The already shallow depth of focus can be very easily amplified through a set of extension rings that can be mounted between the camera body and lens. In combination with the Flektogon 35mm, which can start producing sharp images above 2 cm by default, we are dealing with a very fun makro setup, as can be seen on the :VideoSeek{seconds=97}[video].

As you can :VideoSeek{seconds=6}[see on the video], Helios 44 is a very known for the very __imperfect__ bokeh it produces. To increase the volume of bubbles, working with vegetation does great, trees in direct sunlight are able to create a surreal yet sharp images. 

:br

Flektogon is probably the most supreme construction quality wise. The lens is very sharp, it can focus from a very short distance of 20 cm - for comparison the 1800 EUR Sony FE 35mm f/1.4 GM lens has a minimum focusing distance of 27 cm! Construction of the lens is very solid and apart from the other lens has most contemporary appeture switching mechanism - simple dialing. The lens breathing is also acceptable.

:br

Despite Orestor 100mm used to be manufactured in the East Germany (DDR), which was often the source of innovation and __inspiration__ for soviet engineers, it is construction-wise and quality-wise the last on the list. Yes, it is a very compact lens for a 100 mm focal length but the appeture mechanism works so when you open it all the way to the 2.8, you have to press a spring that will push it back to the f/22, from which you can open back to wider appeture...
However, there indeed is a flare of Orestor that we shall call a feature, and that is it's :VideoSeek{seconds=6}[focus imperfection] on a wide open appeture. It can be tricky to find focus right, because even tho we hit that focus range right, it might feel like like we just missed, because of the very soft aura on the very borderline of the focused object and background.

:br 

Not only this lens trio looks great in a forest during a day, but they have been essential partners during my professional career. With bit of a practise and indeed sharp eye, theese  lenses have been handling potraits, concerts, events and stills better than many of my former digital pieces.

In conclusion there are hundreds of lens with M42 mount that have been manufactured in the last century, infinite spectrum of focal lengths appetures and flares that are waiting to be tried out. If you would be looking for any of another exceptional analogue lens, I would point you to the Carl Zeiss Pancolar which is prominent due its __fire circle__ or member of the Hellios family 85mm that elevates the bubble bokeh introduced here by it's little brother to another dimension.
